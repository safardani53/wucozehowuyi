Face tattoos go mainstream as body art-mad women ink stars, hearts and flowers on their heads

Publish date: 2024-03-04

LOVE them or loathe them, face tattoos are becoming mainstream for women.

With gorgeous stars such as Halsey, Kehlani and Kat Von D all sporting the controversially-placed ink, it's proving that you can sport facial art and look feminine too.

In the past, face tattoos have been linked to gangsters and inking addicts.

So the rise in popularity, especially in women, is a surprising new twist to tattoo trens.

Kat Von D, a tattoo artist made famous on the American show LA Ink who is now known for her cosmetics range, has stars on one side of her face.

They have become an iconic look for ink fans.

As a tattoo artist it's perhaps not unexpected she would have ink covering every part of her body.

But it's not just tattoo artists and very heavily tattooed women who are getting pretty face tattoos.

The trend of getting a simple outline of a shape inked seems to have move from the ankle or the wrist to the head.

The dinky pieces of body art are increasing in popularity and are also becoming less of a taboo and something to be embraced.

Halsey, singer of 'Eastside' and 'Closer' recently unveiled her queen of diamonds tattoo on the side of her face, saying "my first (and hopefully only) face tattoo".

And her good friend R&B singer Kehlani is no stranger to some face ink either, as the stunning star has a dinky paper plane as well as some dots on her face.

We might not be booking our tattoo appointments just yet, but we could be swayed...
