Kirk Douglas is an American actor, producer, director, philanthropist, and writer. After a poor childhood with immigrant parents and six sisters, he made his film debut in Martha Ivers' Strange Love with Barbara Stanwyck. Douglas quickly became a box office star throughout the 1950s, known for his serious television dramas, including Western and war films. During his career, he appeared in over 90 films. Douglas is known for his explosive acting style, which he has portrayed as the merciless Town crime lawyer. Douglas has become an international star thanks to the positive reception for his role as an unscrupulous boxing hero in Champion, earning him his first Oscar nomination for Best Actor.
