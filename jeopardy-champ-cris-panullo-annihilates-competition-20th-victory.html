Jeopardy! super-champ Cris Panullo 'annihilates' competition for 20th straight victory as fans wonde

Publish date: 2024-07-21

JEOPARDY! champion Cris Panullo has crushed the competition in his latest win, walking away with over $20,000 more than other players.

The New Jersey native has won the game show more than 17 times thus far.

During Friday's episode of Jeopardy!, Cris came away with yet another win.

The operations success manager won the show with $44,121 – tens of thousands of dollars more than the other two competitors.

Josh Gregor walked away with just over $1,328 while Elana Friedman won $11,000.

Cris has been raking up wins for several weeks on Jeopardy!, shocking fans along the way.

Fans were thrilled for the star, taking to Reddit and other social media platforms to celebrate him.

One commenter wrote: "Runaway Cris is back baby."

Another commented: "Amazing how in just a single year and a half time, we went from having just 3 contestants who could claim they won 20 or more games on Jeopardy to now 7. More than double in just like 15 months!"

A third fan commented: "Well a classic runaway from Cris to win Game no. 20. Simple as that. And got the FJ right thanks to the classical music part of the clue."

Cris played his 19th game on Thursday and the difficult questions weren't all he had to look out for during the game.

The star also faced some shady comments from another contestant.

Rob Lamanna, a new player, celebrated audibly when Cris missed a question during the show, missing the chance to take an even bigger lead by snagging a Daily Double.

Of Cris' many wins, 15 have been runaways, meaning there was no chance other players could catch up to him by the time they reached Final Jeopardy.

Thursday's game, however, was much closer and Cris struggled in some areas, including a Daily Double he stumbled upon during gameplay.

During the second round, Cris led with $23K while Rob was still in the game with $14K, Jeri with $1.8K

Jeri landed on the last randomly placed "Daily Double" left - meaning Cris couldn't take the W by default getting it correct and betting big.

Before Jeri answered it, Rob turned to her and said "thank you, Jeri."

Host Ken Jennings, 48, heard him and joked: “You kept Cris from finding it!"

Rob nodded sarcastically up and down toward Ken, hinting that was why he was so happy.

Cris made an upset face but didn't say a word.

CRIS CLINCHES VICTORY

Going into Final Jeopardy, Cris finished up with $27,600 to Rob's $14,200 and Jeri's $3,900.

Cris got the Shakespearian clue correct and closed the curtains on his competitors with $28,821, just $2K more than Rob who went for broke with a huge last wager.

Cris now has a 19-day total of $666,744 - only four contestants have ever amassed more.

Eagle-eyed Jeopardy! fans were thrilled he won but left bothered by how Rob handled the mid-game moment. 

One Reddit user wrote: "Rob's smirk and 'Thank you Jeri' when she chose the third Daily Double felt a bit off-putting."

Another wrote: "I agree, but if you look at it charitably, maybe he was thanking Jeri for keeping Cris from getting it? Still, it seemed to be an unnecessary comment to me."

A third wrote: "Oh I absolutely think that's what he meant; that's part of what irked me haha."

A fourth defended: "I think he was just playing really hard in a very unenviable position."

A fifth fan wrote that even if the reasoning behind the gesture was understandable, it was not in good taste to say something.

"Yeah, not a big fan of that too especially since Cris seems like a nice dude, and it borders on bad sportsmanship. 

"But, I'm going to give Rob the benefit of the doubt and just chalk it up to his competitive nature.
