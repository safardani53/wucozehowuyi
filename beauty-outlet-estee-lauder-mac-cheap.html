I went to the viral discount make-up shop and stocked up on some cult faves - people need to run the

Publish date: 2024-06-21

A BEAUTY buff has told people to run to an outlet store that stocks cult favourite brands for super cheap.

Suzanna Stejkeup visited one of The Cosmetic Company Stores and found shelves filled with in-season MAC, Too Faced, Bobbi Brown and Clinique. 

Make-up and skincare products were slashed by more than 50% of what you’d pay at a full-price retailer. 

Recording her visit on Tik Tok, the 22-year-old said: “So we went to The Cosmetic Company Store.

“Everyone is raving about it on Tik Tok right now so we had to just see because I wanna get some Huda Beauty stuff.

“The other girl got like three Huda Beauty powders.

“They didn’t have any Huda Beauty but look at the prices.

“Literally MAC powders for £10, eyeshadows for £12. 

“I ended up getting a lipstick I think for like £14 from MAC - a red one - and an eyeshadow for like £12.”

MAC lipsticks cost £20 full price but were priced at £13.75 in the outlet.

Among the bargains were Too Faced mascaras for £13.50 down from £19 and the company’s iconic Born This Way face powder for £20.50 rather than £29.

Suzanna dubbed the store “so good” but her only grievance was the absence of Huda Beauty products.

She continued: “I love this foundation… The Estee Lauder Double Wear is £26 there.

“So run if you love that foundation.”

Estee Lauder’s Double Wear foundation is a cult classic and costs £38 full price.

Suzanna visited the chain’s shop in The O2 in Greenwich, London.

The outlet store is run by Estee Lauder and stocks most of the parent company’s brands looking to flog surplus products.

A blurb on its website reads: “As part of The Estée Lauder Companies we offer award winning premium skincare, makeup, haircare, and fragrance products.

“From a collection of prestigious brands including Estee Lauder, Clinique and MAC.”
