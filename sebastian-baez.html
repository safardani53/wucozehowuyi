Sebastian Baez: Net Worth &amp; Career [2023 Update]

Publish date: 2024-06-19

Sebastian Baez, an Argentine tennis player, looks forward to Grand Slam titles after his ATP Tour debut in 2021. Starting the 2020 tour outside of the Top 300, he ranked a career-high #32 in the ATP Singles ranking on August 1, 2022.

On March 12, 2018, Sebastian ascended to the #1 spot in the combined ITF junior rankings.

The gold medalist in the 2018 Summer Youth Olympics, Baez records one ATP win (Estoril Open 2022) and six ATP challenger singles, with a career overall of 29-33 singles & 0-6 doubles.

Moreover, the Buenos Aires-born has five ITF titles out of seven appearances, including one in his home court.

Thanks to his impressive accomplishments, Sebastian earned a spot at the 2021 ATP Next Gen Finals when Jenson Brooksby withdrew due to an injury.

The tennis star saw his way to the semi-finals before losing to Carlos Alcaraz, considered #1 in ATP Singles (ranked in November 2022).

Throughout his career, Baez made career earnings of $1,247,486- thanks to his ATP tours & Grand Slam appearances (though yet to win one).

Keeping on reading to know more about Sebastian Baez- his career achievements, prize money, and family.

Sebastian Baez | Early Life & Family

Baez was born in Buenos Aires, Argentina, on December 28, 2000.

The Argentine tennis player prefers to keep his family profile away from the media’s attention. Though, often Sebastian mentions on his Instagram posts that his family was his biggest supporter.

As reported, Sebastian Baez’s mother would take him to tennis classes on a scooter during his early days.

His father could have been a tennis player- Baez took an interest in tennis at 2 years old when he stumbled on a tennis racquet of his dad. Later that hobby turned into his career.

When Sebastian Baez was 10, he drew the attention of Luis, a former Argentine tennis player, who signed him to the Parque Roca academy and offered him to join his squad.

Later in 2016, he would make his tennis debut in ITF Junior tournaments.

Sebastian Baez | ATP Debut In 2021

A runner-up of the 2018 French Open to Tseng Chun-hsin of Taiwan, Baez was considered top-ranked as per the ITF combined ranking (of March 12, 2018).

And three years later, the Argentina-born made his ATP Tour debut in the 2021 Chile Open, where he lost to Holger Vitus Ndskov Rune in straight sets.

At the 2021 Hamburg European Open, Sebastian Baez defeated Frenchman Corentin Moutet in the main draw opener but lost to Georgia-born Nikoloz Basilashvili in the second round.

Moreover, he also faced defeat against Carlos Alcaraz in the semi-final of the 2021 Next Generation ATP Finals in Milan.

He had come as a replacement for Jenson Brooksby, who withdrew due to an abdominal injury.

But those losses only meant the ladder for the victories to come!

Won Six ATP Challenger Tour Titles In The 2021 Season

Sebastian Baez won his first-ever ATP Challenger title in the 2021 Challenger Concepción, leaving Francisco Cerúndolo behind in February 2021.

After further four wins, he became the youngest player with five ATP Challenger Tour titles in a season- with victories in:

  • 2021 Challenger de Santiago (against Tomás Barrios Vera)
  • Zagreb Open 2021 (against Juan Pablo Varillas)
  • 2021 Challenger de Santiago III (defeated Felipe Meligeni Alves)
  • 2021 Challenger de Buenos Aires (defeated Thiago Monteiro)

Moreover, Baez added another Challenger title in November 2021 after winning the 2021 Campeonato Internacional de Tênis de Campinas.

By the end of the 2021 tour, his ATP Singles ranking rose to #97 from #309 in 2020- earning him the first entry into the Top 100.

Sebastian Baez Won First Open Singles In 2022

The year 2022 saw the Argentine player with new challenges- competed in the 2022 Melbourne Summer Set 1 and 2022 Sydney International but returned without trophies.

In his Grand Slam debut at the 2022 Australian Open, he won against Albert Ramos Violas in the first match of the main draw.

But lost in the second round to Stefanos Tsitsipas, who is considered among the 12 top-paid tennis players in the world.

Rafael Nadal won the 2022 Australia Open and took home $2.05 million in prize money.

In April 2022, Sebastian won the 2022 Estoril Open winning 6-3 & 6-2 in the final against Frances Tiafoe (of the United States).

His other ATP tour Open tournaments include:

  • 2022 Córdoba Open (lost to Alejandro Tabilo in quarterfinals)
  • 2022 Argentina Open (faced defeat to Lorenzo Sonego in the second round of the main draw)
  • The 2022 Rio Open (lost to Thiago Monteiro in the main’s first round)
  • The 2022 Chile Open (lost in the final against Pedro Martínez)
  • Swedish Open 2022 (lost to fellow Argentine Francisco Cerúndolo in the final)

Aside from the Australian Open in 2022, Baez competed in other Grand Slam tournaments: US Open, French Open, and Wimbledon Open, finishing only up to the second round.

Moreover, the Tennis player has competed in doubles, too- won in the 2020 ITF World Tennis Tour in Prague, partnering with Pedro Cachín.

Did You Know? In the 2018 Summer Youth Olympics, Baez and Facundo Díaz Acosta won the gold medal for Argentina in the boys’ doubles event.

What Is Baez’s ATP Ranking In 2023?

As of January 2023, Baez ranks #43 in the ATP Singles ranking- his career-high rank was #31 on August 1, 2022.

The top-5 players in the Singles ranking are Carlos Alcaraz, Rafael Nadal, Casper Ruud, Stefanos Tsitsipas, and Novak Djokovic.

He ranked #885 in the ATP Doubles ranking in 2021 but has not played Doubles since.
