Student claims she's found a bizarre 'cure' for the agonising psoriasis left her a recluse - 8 UDD

Publish date: 2024-03-02

A STUDENT whose psoriasis left her a recluse claims her agonising condition was "cured" by an £8 udder cream.

Five years ago Eleanor Cartwright, now 23, had perfect skin but when she developed psoriasis after a bout of tonsillitis her self confidence plummeted.

The biomedical sciences student was covered in scaly red patches within two weeks of the illness and nothing she was prescribed cleared up her itchy skin condition.

Left self-conscious and worried about the way she looked Eleanor stopped going out with her friends and didn't want to date anyone.

She became a recluse, only leaving the house for lectures and hockey practice.

Doctors diagnosed her psoriasis as an immune response to her tonsillitis and prescribed a drug called cyclosporine for three months to help shut down the immune response.

After two years of suffering Eleanor, from Oxfordshire, was still in constant pain and was put back on the drug for another 12 weeks.

But the drug caused her to suffer pins and needles like "going out into the freezing cold and then jumping into a warm bath".

When the drug had no effect for the second time doctors prescribed her something harsher - methotrexate, a form of chemotherapy drug, which made her even more ill.

Desperate to cover up the condition Eleanor would resort to wearing thick black tights in summer and layers of make-up to cover the patches on her face.

"Wearing black tights on a summers day was going to get some sort of reaction but I tried to laugh comments off  and play along to avoid any more questions," she said.

"I also used to wear a full face of make-up to hockey practise which again got people talking."

Eleanor eventually had to put her studies on hold because the pain was affecting her concentration.

It was during one of her classes that she discovered MooGoo products, creams that used to be used on cows' udders but has now been made for humans, and decided to give it a go.

The all-natural product was developed by an Australian company and was originally used to soothe the udders of milk cows.

"We had a whole module based around the drugs I'd been on and I wanted to know more so I was researching them as treatment for psoriasis and how they worked and on one of the search pages I saw a mention of this MooGoo cream that had helped someone else" Eleanor said.

"So I decided to order some because the Methotrexate wasn’t working but I was feeling all of the side effects."

"It was at this point that I thought I might as well try it as I had nothing really left to lose but I had little hope that it would work.

"As a  a young woman my psoriasis prevented me doing a lot, I was struggling with self consciousness and didn’t go out with my friends socialising as it was so bad."

Within months of using the cream Eleanor saw a a difference in her skin.

"Now I am happy to go makeup free once again which is something I never thought would happen," she said.

"I still have a couple of bits on my cheeks but I’m fine with that now.

"During university I had been seeing someone for a few months but when my skin hit a bad patch I just felt too self-conscious and I hit rock bottom and that feeling was the turning point for me, I said to myself I had to get this sorted before even thinking about getting close to a guy again, I just dropped dating altogether.”

"After that I just didn’t even bother with a social life or  a love life- I left the house for lectures for hockey practice and that was it."

"Two to three month into using MooGoo and my skin clearing up I braved downloading Tinder to ease myself back into dating.

"I met my now boyfriend of two and a half years and as they say, the rest is history."

According to the British Association of Dermatologists there is no cure for psoriasis.

"Treatment of psoriasis depends upon your individual circumstances," according to the associations website.

"It may be that you find that treatment applied to the surface of the skin (topical treatment) is sufficient to treat your psoriasis.

"For people with more extensive or difficult to treat psoriasis, light treatment (phototherapy), tablet treatment or injection treatment may be required.

MORE ON PSORIASIS

"The aims of topical psoriasis treatment are to remove excess scaly skin and calm the underlying inflammation.

"This will improve the appearance and help the skin feel more comfortable and less itchy.

"Different treatments are often used at different body sites and for some areas such as the scalp, a combination of treatments are needed to get the best results."

The most common form of topical psoriasis treatment is emollient moisturisers and creams containing vitamins A and D.

MooGoo's Skin Milk Udder Cream was formulated after founder Craig Jones's mother used an old fashioned country remedy to manage her skin condition.

The lotion was used by dairy farmers to keep cows' udders soft and supple but has now been changed for human use.
