John Leslie "Wes" Montgomery is an American jazz guitarist. One of the most influential guitarists of the 20th century, Montgomery is known for his thumb picking technique and ability to use octaves extensively, giving him a distinctive sound. Montgomery often works with his brothers Buddy and Monk and with organist Melvin Rhyne. His recordings up until 1965 were aimed at hard bop, soul jazz and post bop, but around 1965, he began recording more pop instrument albums that were popular with the general public. His guitar style later influenced a combination of smooth jazz and jazz.
