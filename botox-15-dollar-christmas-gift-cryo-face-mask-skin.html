'My friend thought I'd got Botox,' shoppers rave over $15 cryo face mask that 'plumps and hydrates'

Publish date: 2024-04-27

SKINCARE enthusiasts have found a $15 cryo face mask that plumps and hydrates.

It works so effectively, according to some reviews, that people thought users had gotten Botox.

People who tried the cryo rubber face mask with moisturizing hyaluronic acid by Dr. Jart+ were impressed.

Currently, the product retails for $15.

"This mask includes a highly concentrated ampoule with hyaluronic acid and a flexible, skin-cooling mask," its description says.

"It leaves skin feeling soft and replenished while looking healthy and rejuvenated," the description continues.

Dr. Jart+ said their product plumps and hydrates the skin.

The mask has a 4.3 rating out of 600 reviews on Sephora's website.

Eighty-five percent of reviewers said they would recommend the item.

"This is such an amazing firming mask," said one person. "Right after I used it, my friend saw my skin on Facetime and asked if I got Botox."

"They feel absolutely luxurious. This one in particular makes my skin look and feel plump and healthy," a second said.

"Dr.Jart Cryo Rubber are my favorite masks," a third wrote. "Application is easy. Stays on for the duration of time."

Rubber masks are an effective way for ingredients to penetrate the skin, The Zoe Report wrote.

"A rubber mask increases absorption and efficacy of any skin active [ingredients]," Dr. Marnie Nussbaum, MD, a board-certified dermatologist, told The Zoe Report.

Traditional sheet masks are made from made of fiber, cotton, cellulose, or coconut pulp, per The Cut.

However, rubber masks are made out of algae, per Dr. Jart+, which helps with the deeper absorption.
