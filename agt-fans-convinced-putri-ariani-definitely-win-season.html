AGT fans are convinced contestant with jaw-dropping talent will 'definitely' win despite being only

Publish date: 2024-08-28

AMERICA'S Got Talent fans have the Season 18 winner already pegged down.

Even though the season just started two weeks ago, they think the jaw-dropping talent singer, Putri Ariani, will take home the win.

AGT reached its second week of auditions last week and this season is already shaping up to have incredibly talented acts.

Putri is a 17-year-old singer from Indonesia.

She is blind and has aspirations to attend Julliard College.

Putri wowed the judges with her incredible voice, so much so that Simon Cowell gave her the Golden Buzzer.

He was not impressed the first time around, but he allowed her to sing another song and then gave her the special honor.

The singer also won over the hearts of America that night and many have pegged her as the winner.

Her story is similar to Kodi Lee, who also got the Golden Buzzer on Season 14.

He went on to win the season as well as All-Stars in 2023.

Kodi is blind, like Putri, and has Autism, and wowed the judges with his voice as well.

'DEFINITE WINNER'

AGT posted a photo of Putri receiving the Golden Buzzer to their Instagram page.

"@arianinismaputri is a star and @simoncowell's #GoldenBuzzer!" the show captioned the post.

Fans flooded the comments with proclamations of her winning.

"She’s going to win, no doubt," one fan wrote.

To that, someone replied: "I super agree with that statement, well-deserved."

"Rooting for you to win! I think we all are," said another.

"She's the female version of Kodi Lee. Definitely going to win!" commented a third.

"Putri is a winner in my book," wrote an excited fan.

TOUCHING AUDITION

Putri had to audition twice for the judges.

The blind 17-year-old performer took to the piano for a passionate rendition of an original song.

Simon, impressed with Putri's vocal ability, jumped onto the America's Got Talent stage and insisted that she perform another song, causing her to deliver a rendition of Elton John's "Sorry Seems to Be the Hardest Word."

The audience and judges loved both performances, and Howie Mandel said: "A lot of people don’t believe in angels, and I think one just landed on our stage. You’re a superstar."

Meanwhile, Simon, before giving the young performer his Golden Buzzer, said: “You’ve got an amazing, distinctive voice. You have a kind of glow about you.”
