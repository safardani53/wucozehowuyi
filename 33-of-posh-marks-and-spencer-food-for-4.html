I bagged 33 of posh M&amp;S food for 4 - theres loads of carbs but itll keep us fed for a few

Publish date: 2024-08-24

A FOODIE has shared how she managed to bag £33 of mouthwatering food from Marks & Spencer – but only paid £4.

TikTok user Charlotte, who can be found at @charlottegrace98, uploaded a video where she went through her shopping bag and showed its delicious contents – from posh hot dogs to pizzas. 

She then said how she had only handed over £4 for everything inside – and it was clear that it would feed her for both lunch and dinner for a good few days. 

That’s because Charlotte used the popular food app Too Good To Go.

The company – which wants to reduce food waste in the UK – asks its customers to select a Magic Bag filled with an array of products close to their sell-by date.

Marks & Spencer is one of the stores on the app, alongside other supermarkets like Morrisons.

Charlotte explained in the caption to the video: “We are back with another M&S TooGoodToGoBag. 

“As always, this cost me £4, this week’s value was £33.80! AMAZING.”

She then went on to unpack the bag, and went through the items one by one. 

The first thing she showed was a pack of 6 brioche posh dog buns, as well as some pork sausages that could be served inside

Then on to some of the best items in the bag – a salami and roquito pepper woodfired pizza and some loaded potato skins with bacon, cheese and spring onion.

A lot of the other items in the bag were similarly carb heavy.

But Charlotte said that only added to her excitement.

She told her followers: “It’s very carby, but we love carbs in this house.”

The carbs in the bag included the classic mini roll selection, 2 garlic and coriander naan breads, and a farmhouse cheddar cheese and celery sandwich.

There were also some added surprises – two freshly squeezed orange juice, some pulled ham hock and bramley apple hot cross buns.

And it seems Charlotte’s success on the Too Good To Go App, and her videos about it, have inspired her followers to jump on the bandwagon.

After she posted her M&S food haul, one of her followers wrote: “I’m getting my first two too good to go bags today.

She added: “I’m so excited.”
