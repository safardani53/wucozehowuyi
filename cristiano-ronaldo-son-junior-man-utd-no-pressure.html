Man Utd star Ronaldo wont force Cristiano Jr, 11, into football.. but dubs him the future

Publish date: 2024-04-07

CRISTIANO RONALDO has promised 'never to pressure' his mega-talented eldest son into staying in football.

The Manchester United legend labelled 11-year-old Cristiano Jr the 'present and future'.

And Ronaldo, 36, believes his son - now in United's academy - would be a 'great' player if he remained 'dedicated' to the sport like his dad.

Proud Ron left United fans speculating Cristiano Jr could be a potential superstar with Instagram shots of the topless striker training with his son.

But the five-time Ballon d'Or winner emphasised he will encourage his lad to make his own choices.

Ronaldo explained his attitude in girlfriend Georgina Rodriguez's new Netflix series 'I Am Georgina'. 

He said of his son's footballing future: "Only time will tell. I will never pressure him. He will do what he wants. 

"Also what I want the most for Cristianito and everyone else is that they're happy and that they choose what they want.

"I will support in any way."

Ronaldo has a strict regime of physical fitness and emotional well-being as he hopes to play on for another five or six years.

And family time is a key part of his holistic outlook.

Ronaldo said: "The best thing that we can do is have children and educate them to have unconditional love.

"And to have a woman by your side who gives you stability then the kids' education is phenomenal."
