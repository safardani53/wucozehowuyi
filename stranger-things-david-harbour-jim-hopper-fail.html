Publish date: 2024-04-27

THE cast of Stranger Things had low expectations when its first season hit Netflix.

The sci-fi series has blossomed into the streaming service’s most popular show in recent years, but Jim Hopper actor David Harbour admitted that nobody was more surprised than him.

The 44 year old, who is currently dating Lily Allen, revealed that he’d suspected the show would crash and burn upon its release after the first round of filming.

“I was sure it was gonna be a complete disaster and a big failure before it came out,” he said during an interview on The Late Show with Stephen Colbert.

“I remember when we were shooting, too, we would all sit around talking about how terrible it was gonna be, mainly because of my performance. I thought I was tanking the whole show.”

He explained that a friend of his had lead him to believe that the lack of hype in the run up to the show’s launch was a bad sign.

“Before it came out, you normally see things on buses. I live in New York, I’m wandering around, buses, phone booths, there are ads for shows. Not a single ad,” he continued.

“Three weeks before the show, a week before the show, and I was doing a play with a friend of mine who’s on a very successful television program, and I said to him, ‘No ads, no ads. I guess they’re doing some kind of new campaign.’

“He said, ‘No, they’re burying the show.’ And I said, ‘What does that mean, burying the show? I don’t understand your television lingo.’ And he said, ‘They hate the show, they’re trying to make sure no one watches it.’”

Suffice to say David couldn’t have been more wrong.

Season 3 of Stranger Things broke records, with more than 40 million households watching the show In its first four days.

David reflected on the moment he began to feel optimistic about the show’s future.

“Then the show came out and it was an overnight zeitgeist success. I really thought it was gonna be nothing and then people sort of embraced it and it became this grassroots event and it’s very gratifying,” he concluded.

Stranger Things seasons 1-3 are available to stream on Netflix.
