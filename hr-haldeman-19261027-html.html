Harry Robbins "Bob" Haldeman is an American political aide and businessman, best known as President Richard Nixon's chief of staff in the White House and as a result, he became involved in the Watergate scandal. . Born in California, Haldeman served in the Naval Reserve during World War II and studied at UCLA. In 1949 he joined advertising agency J. Walter Thompson, where he worked for 20 years as a leading advertising manager in Los Angeles and New York. He quickly made a name for himself in Los Angeles society through his work as President of the UCLA Alumni Association and a member of the University of California Board of Trustees. His long family ties to Republicans and his own interests drew Haldeman into politics. In the 1950s, he got to know Nixon, with whom he developed both an intense respect and an unwavering loyalty.
