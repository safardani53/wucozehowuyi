Henry Santos is a Dominican artist. Best known as a singer and songwriter in the bachata band Aventura, Henry Santos is a Dominican singer from New York who debuted as a solo artist in 2011. Born Henry Santos Jeter December 15, 1979, in Moca, Espaillat, Dominican Republic, he and his family moved to the Bronx, NY at the age of 13. He's the cousin of bachata star Romeo Santos, whom he co-founded Aventura with in 1996. Originally called "Los Tinelles", the group made their debut with "Trampa De Amor" in 1998. Aventura rose to world fame with their hit "Obsesión" on their third album, We Broke The Rules. Aventura's success was heightened by "Ella Y Yo", a collaboration with reggaeton artist Don Omar on their fifth Studio album, God's Project. This is the first of ten consecutive best songs on the Billboard Hot Latin Songs chart. After nine albums, Aventura announced a split from the group in 2011.
