Ghost of 'black eyed girl' stalking walkers in UK beauty spot 'seen by drone'

Publish date: 2024-08-29

A GHOST known as the 'black eyed girl' has finally been spotted 'by drone' stalking walkers in a popular UK beauty spot.

Paranormal activity professionals have claimed this creepy video could be the first real evidence of a supernatural entity in the area - after years of sightings.

The mysterious footage was captured in Britain's "most haunted" place, Cannock Chase, Staffordshire, and the 'spirit' has been haunting visitors for over four decades.

Robert Pulme is a ghost hunter who believes the "black eyed girl" is real, he said: “Cannock Chase is considered one of the most haunted places in Britain, so it is no surprise the area draws in lots of rumours about beasts and ghouls.

"We have had lots of reports of a ‘black eyed girl’ seen walking around.

"At first she appears like a normal girl and walkers are worried a little girl is lost in the woods.

"Then they see her eyes and she just disappears. It is terrifying.”

The ghost expert believes this girl's spirit may be a child who died from diphtheria in the 1800s as the disease made the under eyes appear sunken and dark.

Another intriguing theory is this ghost has not been able to move into the afterlife because she has unfinished business.

Some believe she was murdered by a serial killer called Raymond Morris - who killed three children in the same area between 1954 and 1967.

But many people believe it is a hoax and say locals dress up deliberately to scare walkers and keep the legend alive.

However, a group of teenagers claim to have spotted the spirit in 2021 when they went camping.

Of the experience, they told Birmingham Live: “I was absolutely terrified.

"I’d read the stories in the news about the black-eyed child, but it wasn’t until the thing stood right in front of me that I could quite believe them.

“I knew instantly that we were dealing with the real thing because it moved in ways humans simply can’t move.

"It was like it could teleport from one place to another when it was moving around and hiding behind the trees.”

The group also saw the little girl the next morning looking at them with her dark eyes from behind a tree.

Lee Brickley, another supernatural activity expert, told the Daily Star: “There was also another incident in October 2019 when a young girl chased after the black eyed child near Castle Ring, and again, she was unable to catch up with her.

"The girl involved ran through the trees following the black eyed child until she reached a sheer cliff-edge drop of around twenty feet.

"Had she not managed to slow her pace and avoid the fall, the girl would have come to serious harm."

Lee Brickley also claimed to have found giant 16 inch footprints in Staffordshire - and considered them proof of the Sasquatch legend.

The 34-year-old said the ferocious claw marks could only belong to Big Foot.
