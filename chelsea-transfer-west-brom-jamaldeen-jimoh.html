Chelsea lead transfer chase for West Brom prodigy Jamaldeen Jimoh, 15, as they target young domestic

Publish date: 2024-04-28

CHELSEA lead the chase for West Brom youth star Jamaldeen Jimoh.

The Stamford Bridge club are on a major recruitment push for young domestic talent.

Jimoh plays for England Under-16s and is part of a Lions squad that already features four other Chelsea kids on their way to the top.

Now Chelsea want the 15-year-old midfield dynamo  —  and hope to do a deal to land him as soon as possible.

Meanwhile, from The Hawthorns to the Bernabeu, Chelsea are also eyeing up Real Madrid centre-back Eder Militao.

The 24-year-old centre back has three years left on his current deal at the Spanish giants.

But he is said to be one of the squad's lowest earning players with an annual salary of £2.5million.

It is thought that both the player and club want to renew the existing contract, but Marca reports that there have been no formal discussions so far.

Militao tops the statistic for centre back with the most duels won in Europe's top five leagues so far this season.

He has featured 34 times in all competitions and been a major reason why Carlo Ancelotti's side sit top of the LaLiga table.

However, with contract talks stalling there is a chance he may look at alternative options in the summer.

And while it is clear Militao does not want to leave - as he enjoys working under Ancelotti - the Brazilian has been eyed up by several of Europe's giants.

Bayern Munich have previously been linked with a move for the South American.

But Chelsea are now thought to be the frontrunners and are set for a meeting with his agent.
